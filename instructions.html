• Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt together in medium bowl. Set aside.
  • Using hand or stand mixer, cream the butter and brown sugar together on medium speed until smooth, roughly 1-2 minutes. Add egg and beat on high until combined, roughly 1-2 minutes. Scrape down sides and bottom of bowl as needed. Add maple syrup, vanilla extract, and maple extract, then beat on high speed until combined.
  • Pour dry ingredients into wet ingredients, then mix on low until combined. Add pecans, then mix on low until combined. Dough should be soft and creamy.
  • Cover and chill the dough for 2 hours in the refrigerator.
  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (177 degrees C). Line 2-3 large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Set aside.
  • Roll cookie dough into balls using cookie scoop or spoons, about 1.5 Tablespoons of dough per cookie. Bake each batch for 12-13 minutes until lightly browned on the sides. The centers will look very soft.
  • Remove from oven. If warm cookies look puffy, lightly bang pan on counter to help deflate cookies and create cracks in cookies. Cool cookies on baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

  • Make the icing: In small saucepan over low heat, melt the butter and maple syrup together, whisking occasionally. Once butter has melted, remove from heat and whisk in confectioners' sugar. Taste and add pinch of salt, if desired. Drizzle over cooled cookies. Icing will set after roughly 1 hour.

  • Cookies stay fresh covered at room temperature for up to 1 week.
